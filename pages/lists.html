--- layout: default title: "Happy Lists!" ---

  1. A polynomial of degree \(n\) can have as many as \(n\) real roots,

  2. A polynomial with odd degree always has at least one real root,

  3. A polynomial with even degree may have no real roots,

  4. The range of a polynomial with odd degree is all of \(\mathbb{R}\),

  5. The range of a polynomial with even degree is never all of \(\mathbb{R}\),

  6. A polynomial has up to \(n-1\) turning points (for \(n\geq2\)), and up to \(n-2\) points of inflection (for \(n\geq3\)),

  7. A polynomial with even degree always has at least one turning point (for \(n\geq2\)),

  8. A polynomial with odd degree always has at least one point of inflection (for \(n\geq3\)).
